Green Navigation develops optimization methods to compute maritime routes using real-time and forecast oceanographic and meteorological data. The objective is to reduce fuel consumption and emissions while preserving operational constraints and navigational safety.

The project integrated variational methods, graph-based route search, robust optimization under uncertainty, and data-driven consumption models. A key technical output was the HADAD algorithm and a hybrid global-local strategy for weather routing.

Pilot studies reported average fuel savings around 5-10%. The research also produced publications in Ocean Engineering (2025) and Computational and Applied Mathematics (2024), and contributed to the launch of the Green Navigation spin-off.
