This collaboration with Omnicom Media Group studied how advertising channels affect weekly sales using real campaign and sales data from a large fast-food franchise.

Instead of simple correlations, the project used a Bayesian dynamic linear state-space model based on the Nerlove-Arrow framework. This made it possible to separate trend, seasonality, and exogenous effects while quantifying uncertainty.

The final output was a practical decision-support workflow for media budget allocation, including trade-offs between expected return and risk. The work was executed through Art. 83 contracts with Universidad Complutense and generated academic outputs on advertising effectiveness and Bayesian factorization methods.
