## Predicting Drifting Buoys

In collaboration with Satlink, we modeled the drift trajectories of fish aggregating devices (FADs) to reduce buoy loss near coastlines and improve deployment planning. The project used daily positions from more than 40,000 drifting buoys and ocean-current forecasts from Copernicus and HYCOM.

We developed a Lagrangian integration framework to forecast drift and also used the proprietary buoy dataset to validate ocean circulation models. This work produced a publication in ICCS 2019 on validating ocean general circulation models with drifting buoy data.

## Tun-AI

After the drift project, we developed Tun-AI to estimate tuna presence and biomass from echosounder buoy signals plus oceanographic variables. The system combined buoy telemetry, satellite data, and historical catch records.

The resulting model was deployed at commercial scale and is reported to be used by more than 1,500 vessels. The project produced peer-reviewed publications in Fisheries Research (2022) and Marine Ecology Progress Series (2023).
